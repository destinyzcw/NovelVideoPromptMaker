The Lantern Road
A short sample novel for testing NovelVideoPromptMaker.


Chapter 1: The Departure

Mira laced her boots in the grey light before dawn and stepped out of the cottage.
The village was silent, chimneys cold, the dirt road pale under a sky the color of ash.
She shouldered her pack, heavy with bread and a single brass lantern, and did not look
back at the door she might never open again.

At the edge of the wheat fields she paused. A wind moved through the stalks, bending
them all one way, and somewhere a lone crow called. Her hands were steady though her
heart was not. "I will come back," she said to no one, and the words fell flat in the
empty air. Then she walked on, into the treeline where the road narrowed and the
morning had not yet reached.


Chapter 2: The Crossing

By midday the forest gave way to a river, wide and brown and loud. The old bridge had
rotted; only two ropes and a few slick planks remained, swaying above the current.
Mira tested the first plank with her toe. It groaned but held.

She crossed one careful step at a time, gripping the wet rope until her knuckles ached,
the water roaring below. Halfway across a plank cracked and dropped away, and she froze,
one foot over nothing, breath caught hard in her chest. For a long moment she simply
clung there, trembling. Then she found the next board with her boot and moved again,
slow and certain, until at last she stumbled onto the far bank and lay in the mud,
laughing and gasping at the bright noon sky.
