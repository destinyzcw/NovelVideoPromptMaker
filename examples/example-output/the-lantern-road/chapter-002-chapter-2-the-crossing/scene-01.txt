A handheld shot watches from the far bank across a wide brown river, framing the young woman low as she crouches and reaches a boot toward the first plank of a rotted rope bridge. The camera drifts unsteadily and tightens on her toe pressing the slick, groaning board, then tilts up to her white-knuckled hand clamped on the wet rope — the heavy pack dragging at her back, patched red cloak whipping toward the lens. Only two ropes and a few wet boards sway between her and the camera over the churning current. She mutters under her breath toward the crossing, "Hold for me. Just hold." Bright flat noon light, cold spray glinting between her and the lens, muted greens and browns, as [the river roars and churns, the old plank groans under her boot, wind buffets across open water].
